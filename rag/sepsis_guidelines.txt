## Surviving Sepsis Campaign: International Guidelines for Management of Sepsis and Septic Shock (2021)

### Definition
Sepsis is a life-threatening organ dysfunction caused by a dysregulated host response to infection. Septic shock is a subset of sepsis with circulatory and cellular/metabolic dysfunction associated with a higher risk of mortality.

### Early Recognition Criteria (qSOFA)
- Systolic blood pressure ≤ 100 mmHg
- Respiratory rate ≥ 22 breaths/min
- Altered mental status (Glasgow Coma Scale < 15)
A qSOFA score ≥ 2 suggests possible sepsis and warrants further investigation.

### SOFA Score Components
- PaO2/FiO2 ratio (respiratory)
- Platelet count (coagulation)
- Bilirubin level (liver)
- Mean arterial pressure or vasopressor requirement (cardiovascular)
- Glasgow Coma Scale (neurological)
- Creatinine or urine output (renal)

### Hour-1 Bundle (Initial Resuscitation)
1. Measure lactate level. Re-measure lactate if initial lactate is elevated (> 2 mmol/L).
2. Obtain blood cultures before administering antibiotics.
3. Administer broad-spectrum antibiotics within 1 hour of recognition.
4. Begin rapid administration of 30 mL/kg crystalloid for hypotension or lactate ≥ 4 mmol/L.
5. Apply vasopressors if patient is hypotensive during or after fluid resuscitation to maintain MAP ≥ 65 mmHg.

### Antibiotic Therapy
- Administer IV antimicrobials as soon as possible, ideally within 1 hour of sepsis recognition.
- Use empiric broad-spectrum therapy with one or more antimicrobials to cover all likely pathogens.
- Narrow antimicrobial therapy once pathogen identification and sensitivities are established.
- Suggest daily assessment for de-escalation of antimicrobial therapy.
- Typical duration of therapy: 7-10 days for most infections.

### Fluid Resuscitation
- Use crystalloids as the fluid of choice for initial resuscitation and subsequent volume replacement.
- Suggested initial bolus: 30 mL/kg of IV crystalloid fluid within the first 3 hours.
- Use balanced crystalloids (e.g., Lactated Ringer's) over normal saline.
- Albumin may be used in addition to crystalloids when patients require substantial amounts of crystalloids.
- Avoid hydroxyethyl starches (HES).

### Vasopressor Therapy
- First-line vasopressor: Norepinephrine (target MAP ≥ 65 mmHg).
- Add vasopressin (up to 0.03 U/min) as second-line agent if MAP target not achieved.
- Add epinephrine as third-line agent if needed.
- Use dobutamine for patients with persistent hypoperfusion despite adequate fluid loading and vasopressor use.
- Avoid dopamine except in highly selected patients (e.g., bradycardia).

### Corticosteroids
- Suggest IV hydrocortisone (200 mg/day) for septic shock patients who require ongoing vasopressor therapy.
- Do not use corticosteroids for sepsis without shock.

### Blood Products
- Transfuse red blood cells only when hemoglobin concentration decreases to < 7.0 g/dL in adults (in the absence of extenuating circumstances such as myocardial ischemia, severe hypoxemia, or acute hemorrhage).
- Do not use erythropoietin for treatment of sepsis-related anemia.
- Do not use fresh frozen plasma to correct clotting abnormalities in the absence of bleeding or planned invasive procedures.

### Mechanical Ventilation (for Sepsis-Induced ARDS)
- Use low tidal volume ventilation (6 mL/kg predicted body weight).
- Target plateau pressure < 30 cm H2O.
- Use higher PEEP over lower PEEP for moderate-severe ARDS.
- Use prone positioning for > 12 hours/day in severe ARDS (PaO2/FiO2 < 150).
- Avoid routine use of high-frequency oscillatory ventilation.
- Use neuromuscular blocking agents for ≤ 48 hours in early severe ARDS.

### Source Control
- Identify and treat the specific anatomic source of infection as rapidly as possible.
- Remove intravascular access devices that are a possible source of sepsis promptly after other vascular access has been established.

### Glucose Control
- Use a protocolized approach to blood glucose management.
- Commence insulin dosing when two consecutive blood glucose levels are > 180 mg/dL.
- Target blood glucose ≤ 180 mg/dL rather than ≤ 110 mg/dL.
- Monitor blood glucose every 1-2 hours until stable, then every 4 hours.

### Renal Replacement Therapy
- Use continuous or intermittent renal replacement therapy in sepsis patients with acute kidney injury.
- Use continuous therapies to facilitate management of fluid balance in hemodynamically unstable patients.

### DVT Prophylaxis
- Use pharmacologic prophylaxis (unfractionated heparin or low-molecular-weight heparin) against venous thromboembolism unless contraindicated.
- Use low-molecular-weight heparin over unfractionated heparin when possible.

### Stress Ulcer Prophylaxis
- Give stress ulcer prophylaxis to patients with risk factors for GI bleeding.
- Use proton pump inhibitors or H2 receptor antagonists.

### Nutrition
- Initiate early enteral nutrition (within 72 hours) rather than parenteral nutrition or no nutrition.
- Avoid mandatory full caloric feeding in the first week. Advance as tolerated.

### Lactate as a Marker
- Elevated lactate (> 2 mmol/L) is a key marker of tissue hypoperfusion in sepsis.
- Serial lactate measurements guide resuscitation. Target: lactate normalization (clearance > 10% every 2 hours).
- Lactate > 4 mmol/L is associated with high mortality risk.

### Vital Sign Thresholds Suggesting Sepsis Deterioration
- Heart rate > 90 bpm (tachycardia)
- Temperature > 38.3°C or < 36°C
- Respiratory rate > 20 breaths/min
- Systolic BP < 100 mmHg
- MAP < 65 mmHg
- SpO2 < 92%
- Altered mental status

### Risk Factors for Sepsis
- Age > 65 or very young (neonates)
- Immunocompromised state (chemotherapy, organ transplant, HIV/AIDS)
- Chronic diseases (diabetes, COPD, chronic kidney disease, liver disease)
- Invasive devices (central lines, urinary catheters, mechanical ventilation)
- Recent surgery or hospitalization
- Burns or major trauma

### Organ Dysfunction Indicators
- Acute change in total SOFA score ≥ 2 points
- Acute kidney injury: creatinine > 2.0 mg/dL or urine output < 0.5 mL/kg/hr for 6 hours
- Hepatic dysfunction: bilirubin > 2 mg/dL
- Coagulopathy: platelets < 100,000/μL or INR > 1.5
- Respiratory failure: PaO2/FiO2 < 300
- Cardiovascular: MAP < 70 mmHg or need for vasopressors

### Sepsis Screening Tools
- SIRS criteria (≥ 2 of: temp > 38°C or < 36°C, HR > 90, RR > 20, WBC > 12k or < 4k)
- qSOFA score (bedside: SBP ≤ 100, RR ≥ 22, altered mentation)
- NEWS (National Early Warning Score)
- MEWS (Modified Early Warning Score)
- SOFA score (for ICU patients)
